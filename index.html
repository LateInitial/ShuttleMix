How rankings work
1
Points — Your pair's score in each match is added to your total. Win 21–15 and you earn 21 pts; your opponents earn 15 pts each. More points = higher rank.
2
Wins — If two players have equal points, the one with more match wins ranks higher.
3
Points difference — Still tied? Your total score margin across all games (win 21–15 is +6, lose it is −6). A higher difference ranks higher.
1
Adjusted points — Players who've played fewer games are topped up so totals are comparable. Each missing game is credited at the group's average points per game.
2
The maths — Adjusted = your points + (most games anyone played − your games) × average points per game. A * marks anyone who got a top-up.
3
Tiebreakers — Equal adjusted points? More wins ranks higher, then points difference.
1
Points per game — Your total points divided by games played, so the ranking stays fair even when people play different numbers of games.
2
Wins — Equal points per game? The player with more wins ranks higher.
3
Points difference — Still tied? The player with the better total score margin ranks higher.
1
League points — If your pair wins the match, each player on that pair earns 3 points. The losing pair earns 1 point each.
2
Wins — Match wins (not draws) are tracked separately. If league points are tied, more wins ranks higher.
3
Points difference — Still tied? The player with the better total score margin ranks higher.
